Cosmetic Contouring

Subtle yet transformative — reshape uneven tooth edges, smooth rough surfaces, and create perfect symmetry in a single painless visit.

About This Treatment

The Art of Tooth Reshaping

Cosmetic contouring, also known as enameloplasty or tooth reshaping, is one of the most conservative and affordable cosmetic dental procedures available. By carefully removing tiny amounts of enamel, your dentist can reshape pointed canines, smooth jagged edges, round off sharp corners, and create symmetrical tooth lengths across your smile.

This quick, painless procedure requires no anaesthesia and can be completed in a single 30–45 minute visit. It is often combined with dental bonding (adding composite resin) for more dramatic reshaping where needed. The result is a subtly refined, more harmonious smile that looks completely natural.

  • Completely painless — no injections needed
  • Completed in a single 30–45 minute visit
  • No recovery time required
  • Most affordable cosmetic dental procedure
  • Can be combined with bonding for enhanced results
How It Works

The Contouring Process

1

Smile Analysis

Your dentist examines your teeth, takes photographs, and discusses which teeth would benefit from reshaping. X-rays ensure adequate enamel thickness.

2

Marking & Planning

Target areas are marked with a dental pen. Your dentist maps out exactly where enamel will be removed to achieve optimal symmetry and balance.

3

Precision Reshaping

Using fine diamond instruments and polishing discs, tiny amounts of enamel are sculpted away. Composite bonding is added where teeth need building up.

4

Polish & Finish

Teeth are smoothed, polished to a high shine, and checked for symmetry. You leave with a more refined, balanced smile in just one visit.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Does cosmetic contouring damage the teeth?

No, when performed by an experienced dentist, cosmetic contouring is completely safe. Only a very small amount of enamel (fractions of a millimetre) is removed. Your dentist will first verify with X-rays that you have adequate enamel thickness to safely undergo the procedure.

Is the procedure painful?

No, cosmetic contouring is completely painless. Since only the outermost layer of enamel is gently shaped, there are no nerve endings involved. No anaesthesia or numbing is required. Most patients find the procedure quite comfortable.

What issues can contouring fix?

Cosmetic contouring can fix minor chips, smooth jagged or rough edges, round overly pointed canines, improve slightly overlapping teeth, correct minor bite issues, and even out unequal tooth lengths. For more significant reshaping, it is often combined with composite bonding.
