What Are Digital RVG X-Rays?

RVG (RadioVisioGraphy) is a digital intraoral X-ray system that uses a small electronic sensor placed inside the mouth to capture detailed images of individual teeth, their roots, surrounding bone, and adjacent structures. Unlike traditional film X-rays, RVG produces instant high-resolution digital images with up to 90% less radiation.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we use premium Carestream and Vatech RVG sensors that deliver exceptional image clarity. The digital format allows our dentists to magnify, enhance contrast, and measure structures precisely on screen, leading to more accurate diagnoses and better treatment outcomes.

Benefits of Digital RVG

90% less radiation than conventional film X-rays
Instant image display — no waiting for film processing
Superior resolution for detecting early cavities
Digital zoom and contrast enhancement tools
Eco-friendly — no chemical waste or film disposal
Easy digital sharing with specialists via email

The RVG X-Ray Process

1

Sensor Placement

A small, comfortable digital sensor is positioned next to the tooth area that needs imaging inside your mouth.

2

Quick Exposure

The X-ray tube is aligned and a brief exposure (less than 0.2 seconds) captures the image digitally.

3

Instant Display

The high-resolution image appears on the monitor within seconds for your dentist to analyse.

4

Diagnosis & Discussion

Your dentist reviews the image with you, pointing out findings and explaining the recommended treatment plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Modern RVG sensors are thin, smooth, and rounded at the edges for patient comfort. While some patients find them slightly bulky compared to film, the exposure time is much shorter (under 0.2 seconds), making the overall experience quicker and more comfortable.

RVG X-rays can detect cavities (even between teeth), bone loss from gum disease, tooth root infections and abscesses, cysts, impacted teeth, root fractures, and the condition of existing fillings, crowns, and root canal treatments. They are essential for accurate diagnosis.

Digital RVG is superior in virtually every way: 90% less radiation, instant results (no 5-minute film processing), enhanced diagnostic tools (zoom, contrast, measurement), permanent digital storage, easy sharing, and no toxic chemical waste from film development.
