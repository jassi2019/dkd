Why Scaling & Polishing Matters

Even with diligent brushing and flossing, plaque and tartar accumulate in hard-to-reach areas over time. This hardened calculus cannot be removed with a toothbrush and harbours millions of bacteria that cause gum disease, cavities, and bad breath.

Professional scaling uses ultrasonic vibrations to break apart and remove calculus deposits above and below the gum line. Polishing then smooths the tooth surfaces, removing surface stains and making it harder for plaque to re-accumulate. The result is cleaner teeth, healthier gums, and fresher breath.

Prevents gum disease, cavities, and tooth loss

The Cleaning Process

1

Oral Examination

Your dentist examines your teeth and gums, checking for calculus deposits, gum inflammation, and areas of concern.

2

Ultrasonic Scaling

A Piezon ultrasonic scaler emits gentle vibrations to efficiently break apart and flush away tartar from all tooth surfaces and gum pockets.

3

Hand Scaling

Specialised curettes are used to remove any remaining deposits, especially in areas where ultrasonic instruments cannot reach.

4

Polish & Fluoride

A rubber cup with prophylaxis paste polishes all surfaces to a smooth finish, followed by a fluoride application to strengthen enamel.
