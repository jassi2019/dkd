Why Oral Cancer Screening Matters

India has one of the highest rates of oral cancer in the world, accounting for nearly one-third of all cancers in the country. When detected early, the five-year survival rate for oral cancer exceeds 80%. However, most cases are diagnosed at advanced stages when treatment outcomes are significantly poorer.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we incorporate oral cancer screening into every comprehensive dental examination. Using advanced fluorescence visualization technology alongside thorough clinical examination, our specialists can detect suspicious lesions, pre-cancerous changes, and abnormalities that may require further investigation.

Our Screening Process

A thorough, non-invasive examination that could save your life

1

Risk Assessment

We review your medical history, lifestyle habits including tobacco and alcohol use, family history, and any symptoms such as persistent sores, lumps, or colour changes in the mouth.

2

Clinical Examination

A comprehensive visual and tactile examination of the lips, tongue, cheeks, floor of mouth, palate, gums, and throat. Lymph nodes in the neck are also palpated for abnormalities.

3

Fluorescence Screening

VELscope technology uses natural tissue fluorescence to reveal abnormal cellular changes invisible to the naked eye, identifying pre-cancerous and cancerous lesions at their earliest stage.

4

Biopsy & Referral

If suspicious areas are found, a painless brush biopsy or incisional biopsy is performed. Results guide further treatment, with immediate referral to oncology specialists when needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everyone should have regular oral cancer screenings as part of their dental check-ups. Those at higher risk, including tobacco users, heavy alcohol consumers, individuals with HPV, those with a history of prolonged sun exposure, and anyone over 40, should be screened more frequently. Early detection dramatically improves treatment success.

Warning signs include a sore or ulcer that does not heal within two weeks, red or white patches on the gums, tongue, or lining of the mouth, unexplained bleeding, numbness, persistent earache, difficulty swallowing or chewing, a lump in the neck, and dramatic weight loss. If you notice any of these symptoms, schedule a screening immediately.
