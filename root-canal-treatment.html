Advanced microscope-assisted root canal therapy completed in a single sitting with zero-pain technology. Save your natural tooth, eliminate infection, and restore comfort.

About This Treatment

Modern Root Canals are Pain-Free

Root canal treatment (endodontic therapy) saves severely infected or damaged teeth by removing the diseased nerve tissue from inside the tooth, disinfecting the canals, and sealing them permanently. Contrary to common belief, modern root canal procedures are virtually painless and no more uncomfortable than getting a filling.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we use advanced dental operating microscopes that provide 25x magnification, allowing our endodontists to see and clean every canal with exceptional precision. Combined with rotary NiTi instruments, apex locators, and 3D imaging, our single-sitting root canals deliver predictable, long-lasting results with minimal discomfort.

  • Single-sitting completion for most cases
  • Dental microscope for 25x magnification
  • Rotary NiTi instruments for precision cleaning
  • 3D CBCT imaging for complex anatomy
  • Virtually pain-free with advanced anaesthesia
How It Works

The Root Canal Process

1

Diagnosis & Imaging

Digital X-ray or 3D CBCT scan to assess infection extent and map the root canal anatomy. Local anaesthesia ensures complete numbness before we begin.

2

Access & Cleaning

Using the dental microscope, a small access opening is made. Rotary NiTi files carefully remove infected nerve tissue and bacteria from every canal.

3

Disinfection & Shaping

Canals are shaped and irrigated with antimicrobial solutions to eliminate all bacteria. Ultrasonic activation ensures thorough disinfection of the canal system.

4

Filling & Crown

Canals are sealed with biocompatible material. A custom-made ceramic crown is placed to protect and restore the tooth to full function and appearance.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is root canal treatment really painless?

Yes, with modern anaesthesia techniques, root canal treatment is virtually painless. Most patients compare the experience to getting a routine filling. In fact, the root canal relieves the severe pain caused by the infected nerve. You may experience mild soreness for a day or two after treatment, which is easily managed with over-the-counter medication.

Can a root canal be done in one visit?

Yes, the majority of root canal cases at our clinic are completed in a single sitting lasting 45–90 minutes. Some complex cases with severe infection or curved canals may require two visits. Our endodontists will inform you during the initial assessment if your case requires multiple appointments.

Do I need a crown after a root canal?

In most cases, yes. A root canal-treated tooth becomes more brittle over time because it no longer receives blood supply. A crown protects the tooth from fracture and restores its full strength. For back teeth (premolars and molars), a crown is almost always recommended. Front teeth may sometimes be restored with just a filling.
