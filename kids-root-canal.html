What Is a Kids Root Canal (Pulpectomy)?

A pulpectomy is a root canal treatment specifically designed for baby teeth. When a cavity reaches the nerve (pulp) of a baby tooth, causing pain or infection, a pulpectomy removes the infected pulp, disinfects the canals, and fills them with a resorbable material that dissolves naturally as the baby tooth sheds.

This procedure is essential because baby teeth serve as natural space maintainers for permanent teeth. Losing them prematurely due to infection can lead to crowding, misalignment, and bite problems that require expensive orthodontic treatment later.

The Pulpectomy Process

1

Diagnosis & X-Ray

Digital X-ray reveals the extent of infection and confirms whether pulpectomy is the right treatment for your child.

2

Gentle Numbing

Topical gel and child-safe local anaesthesia ensure your child feels absolutely no pain during the procedure.

3

Pulp Removal & Cleaning

Infected pulp tissue is carefully removed and the root canals are cleaned and disinfected thoroughly.

4

Fill & Restore

Canals are filled with bio-resorbable material and the tooth is restored with a stainless steel or tooth-coloured crown.
