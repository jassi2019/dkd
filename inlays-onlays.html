Ceramic Inlays & Onlays

Premium lab-crafted ceramic restorations that offer the perfect balance between a filling and a crown — preserving maximum healthy tooth structure while restoring strength and beauty.

About This Treatment

The Conservative Alternative

Ceramic inlays and onlays are indirect restorations custom-fabricated in a dental laboratory to precisely fit within or over the damaged portion of a tooth. An inlay fits within the cusps (bumps) of a back tooth, while an onlay extends over one or more cusps for broader coverage.

These restorations are ideal when a cavity is too large for a standard filling but the tooth still has enough healthy structure to avoid a full crown. Made from high-strength porcelain or pressed ceramic (E.max), inlays and onlays are incredibly durable, stain-resistant, and provide a seamless, natural appearance that is virtually indistinguishable from your real tooth.

  • Preserves more healthy tooth than a crown
  • Stronger and more durable than composite fillings
  • Custom-crafted in the lab for precise fit
  • Stain-resistant E.max or zirconia ceramics
  • Lifespan of 15–20 years with proper care
How It Works

The Inlay/Onlay Process

1

Decay Removal & Preparation

Damaged or decayed tooth material is carefully removed. The tooth is shaped to receive the inlay or onlay restoration with precise margins.

2

Digital Impression

A precise digital scan or traditional impression captures every detail of the prepared tooth. This data is sent to our ceramics laboratory for fabrication.

3

Lab Fabrication

Our master ceramists handcraft your inlay or onlay from premium E.max or zirconia ceramic, perfectly matching the shade and anatomy of your natural tooth.

4

Bonding & Finishing

The restoration is tried in, adjusted for perfect fit and bite, then permanently bonded using advanced adhesive cement. Final polishing ensures a seamless result.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between an inlay and an onlay?

An inlay fits within the grooves and cusps of a tooth, similar to a filling but lab-made for superior strength. An onlay is larger, covering one or more cusps of the tooth. Think of an inlay as an "inside" restoration and an onlay as a "cap" over the damaged cusp. Both preserve more tooth structure than a full crown.

Why choose an inlay over a regular filling?

For large cavities, composite fillings can shrink and fracture over time because they are placed directly and cured in the mouth. Lab-fabricated inlays are stronger, more precise, and more durable because they are cured under controlled laboratory conditions. They also offer superior marginal adaptation, reducing the risk of secondary decay.

How long do ceramic inlays and onlays last?

Ceramic inlays and onlays are among the most durable dental restorations available, typically lasting 15–20 years or longer with good oral hygiene. Their longevity significantly exceeds that of direct composite fillings, making them an excellent long-term investment for your dental health.
