Understanding Bad Breath

Halitosis, commonly known as bad breath, affects an estimated 1 in 4 people and can significantly impact social confidence and quality of life. While occasional bad breath from food is normal, persistent halitosis usually signals an underlying dental or medical condition that needs professional attention.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we take a systematic, evidence-based approach to diagnosing and treating bad breath. Over 85% of halitosis cases originate in the oral cavity — from gum disease, tongue bacteria, untreated cavities, or poor dental restorations. Our comprehensive assessment identifies the exact source and provides targeted treatment for lasting freshness.

Frequently Asked Questions

The most common causes are gum (periodontal) disease, bacteria on the tongue surface, untreated cavities, poorly fitting dental restorations, and dry mouth. Less commonly, it can be caused by sinus infections, acid reflux, or other systemic conditions. A professional assessment is essential to identify the specific cause.

Most commercial mouthwashes only mask bad breath temporarily. They don't treat the underlying cause. Some alcohol-based rinses can actually worsen the problem by drying out the mouth. Prescription antimicrobial rinses can help as part of a comprehensive treatment plan, but addressing the root cause is essential for permanent results.

Simple cases involving tartar buildup or surface bacteria can be resolved in 1-2 visits. More complex cases involving gum disease may require multiple visits over several weeks. We design a treatment plan tailored to your specific condition and monitor progress until the issue is fully resolved.
