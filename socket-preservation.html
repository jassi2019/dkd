Socket Preservation Therapy

Protect your jawbone after extraction to ensure successful future implant placement

Home Treatments Gums Treatment Socket Preservation

Why Socket Preservation Matters

When a tooth is extracted, the surrounding bone begins to resorb rapidly. Studies show that up to 50% of the bone width can be lost within the first 12 months after extraction if the socket is left to heal on its own. This bone loss can compromise your ability to receive dental implants in the future and cause adjacent teeth to shift.

Socket preservation involves placing bone graft material into the extraction site immediately after the tooth is removed. This maintains the ridge dimensions, prevents the collapse of surrounding bone, and creates an ideal foundation for a dental implant when you are ready. It is a simple, proactive step that saves you from more extensive and costly bone grafting procedures later.

Prevents up to 50% bone loss after extraction
Maintains ridge width and height for future implants
Performed immediately after extraction in the same visit
Biocompatible graft materials (synthetic, bovine, allograft)
Collagen membrane placed for guided healing
Eliminates need for future bone augmentation surgery

The Preservation Process

1

Atraumatic Extraction

The tooth is carefully removed using minimally invasive techniques to preserve as much surrounding bone and soft tissue as possible.

2

Socket Debridement

The empty socket is thoroughly cleaned of any infection, granulation tissue, or debris to create a sterile environment for grafting.

3

Bone Graft Placement

Medical-grade bone graft material is packed into the socket, filling the void left by the extracted tooth and providing a scaffold for new bone growth.

4

Membrane & Closure

A resorbable collagen membrane is placed over the graft, and the site is sutured. The graft integrates over 3-4 months, forming solid new bone.
