What Is Microscopic Dentistry?

Microscopic dentistry utilizes high-powered dental operating microscopes that provide up to 25x magnification and intense coaxial illumination. This allows our specialists to visualize tooth structures, root canals, cracks, and lesions that are invisible to the naked eye, resulting in more accurate diagnoses and precisely executed treatments.

Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry is equipped with Carl Zeiss and Global surgical microscopes across multiple operatories. Our endodontists, periodontists, and restorative specialists are trained in microscope-enhanced dentistry, ensuring every procedure benefits from this game-changing technology.

How Microscopic Dentistry Works

See more. Treat better. Heal faster.

1

Magnified Diagnosis

The dental microscope reveals hidden fractures, extra root canals, calcified canals, and early decay that standard examination and X-rays may miss entirely.

2

Precision Treatment Planning

With detailed visualization, our specialists create an exact treatment plan targeting only the affected area while preserving maximum healthy tooth structure.

3

Microscope-Guided Procedure

The entire treatment is performed under continuous magnification, allowing sub-millimetre accuracy for root canal shaping, crack detection, or micro-surgical interventions.

4

Documentation & Follow-Up

High-definition video and images of your procedure are captured through the microscope for records and review, ensuring transparent communication about your treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

A dental microscope provides up to 25x magnification, allowing the dentist to see intricate details invisible to the naked eye. This leads to more accurate diagnoses, more conservative treatment preserving healthy tooth structure, higher success rates especially in root canal therapy, and earlier detection of problems like cracks.

Root canal treatments, root canal retreatments, apicoectomy (surgical endodontics), detection of cracked teeth, removal of broken instruments from canals, periodontal microsurgery, and precision restorative work all benefit significantly from microscope magnification. It is particularly valuable for complex and retreatment cases.

There is typically a modest premium for microscope-enhanced procedures due to the advanced equipment and specialized training required. However, the improved success rates and reduced need for retreatment make it a cost-effective investment in the long run. We provide transparent pricing during your consultation.
