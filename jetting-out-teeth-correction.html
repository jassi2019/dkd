Jetting Out Teeth Correction

Correct protruding or flared front teeth to achieve a harmonious, balanced smile and improved facial profile — often in just one week.

About This Treatment

Bring Protruding Teeth Back in Line

Protruding or "jetting out" front teeth (also known as proclination or buck teeth) is a common dental concern that affects both smile aesthetics and facial harmony. This condition can make it difficult to close the lips comfortably and often impacts self-confidence significantly.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we offer both cosmetic and orthodontic solutions depending on the severity of protrusion. For mild to moderate cases, porcelain crowns and veneers can visually reposition the teeth in just two visits. For more severe protrusion, we may combine orthodontic treatment with cosmetic finishing to deliver the best long-term results.

  • Corrects mild to moderate protrusion in 5–7 days
  • Improves facial profile and lip closure
  • All-ceramic crowns for natural aesthetics
  • Digital treatment planning for predictable results
  • Combined ortho-cosmetic approach for severe cases
How It Works

The Correction Process

1

Clinical Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation including X-rays and profile analysis to measure the degree of protrusion and plan the ideal correction approach.

2

Treatment Planning

Based on severity, we recommend veneers/crowns for mild cases or a combination of orthodontic alignment followed by cosmetic finishing for severe cases.

3

Tooth Preparation & Fabrication

Teeth are carefully reshaped and impressions taken. Our lab creates all-ceramic restorations designed to bring the teeth into an ideal position visually.

4

Placement & Profile Check

Final restorations are bonded and your profile is evaluated to ensure harmonious alignment. Fine adjustments are made for perfect lip closure and aesthetics.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Can protruding teeth be fixed without braces?

Yes, for mild to moderate protrusion, porcelain crowns can visually reposition the teeth without braces. The crowns are angled to create the appearance of properly aligned teeth. However, for severe protrusion involving skeletal issues, orthodontic treatment may be necessary first.

Will this treatment change my facial profile?

Yes, correcting protruding teeth often has a positive impact on your facial profile. By bringing the front teeth back into a more natural position, the lips can close more comfortably, and the overall facial balance improves significantly.

How long does the cosmetic correction take?

The cosmetic approach using porcelain crowns typically requires 2 visits over 5–7 days. If orthodontic pre-treatment is needed, the total timeline may be 6–12 months, followed by cosmetic finishing. We will provide a detailed timeline during your consultation.
