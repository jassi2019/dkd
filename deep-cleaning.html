What Is Deep Cleaning?

Deep cleaning, clinically known as scaling and root planing (SRP), goes beyond regular cleaning to treat periodontal disease. While routine scaling addresses deposits above the gum line, deep cleaning reaches below the gum line into periodontal pockets where bacteria, toxins, and calculus have accumulated.

Root planing involves smoothing the root surfaces to remove bacterial toxins embedded in the cementum. This smooth surface encourages the gums to reattach to the tooth, reducing pocket depth and halting the progression of bone loss. Deep cleaning is the gold-standard non-surgical treatment for moderate periodontitis.

Treats gum pockets deeper than 4mm
Halts bone loss and disease progression
Eliminates subgingival bacteria and toxins
Promotes natural gum reattachment
Non-surgical, minimally invasive approach
Local anaesthesia for complete comfort

The Deep Cleaning Process

1

Periodontal Charting

Your periodontist measures pocket depths around every tooth using a calibrated probe, creating a detailed map of disease severity.

2

Local Anaesthesia

The treatment area is thoroughly numbed with local anaesthesia to ensure you remain completely comfortable throughout the procedure.

3

Scaling & Root Planing

Ultrasonic and hand instruments meticulously clean calculus and bacteria from root surfaces deep within periodontal pockets.

4

Re-evaluation

After 4-6 weeks of healing, pocket depths are re-measured to assess gum reattachment and determine if additional treatment is needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Regular cleaning (prophylaxis) removes plaque and tartar above the gum line and is preventive in nature. Deep cleaning goes below the gum line into periodontal pockets, smoothing root surfaces to treat active gum disease. It requires local anaesthesia and is a therapeutic procedure.

Typically, the mouth is divided into quadrants and treated over 2 visits, with two quadrants per session. This allows us to be thorough while keeping each appointment comfortable. Severe cases may require additional sessions with laser-assisted therapy.

You may experience mild soreness and sensitivity for 3-5 days after treatment. Your gums may appear slightly receded as swelling reduces and they tighten around the teeth. This is normal and indicates healing. We provide detailed aftercare instructions and a follow-up appointment.
