What Is Sinus Lift Surgery?

A sinus lift, also called sinus augmentation, is a surgical procedure that adds bone to the upper jaw in the area of the premolars and molars. The sinus membrane is gently lifted upward and bone graft material is packed into the space created, providing the necessary bone height for successful dental implant placement.

This procedure is essential when the upper jawbone has insufficient height due to natural anatomy, bone resorption after tooth loss, or an enlarged sinus cavity. Our experienced oral surgeons use advanced 3D imaging and microsurgical techniques to perform sinus lifts with exceptional precision and predictability.

The Sinus Lift Process

Careful planning and precise execution for predictable outcomes

1

3D Assessment

Comprehensive 3D CBCT scans evaluate sinus anatomy, bone height, and density to create a detailed surgical plan for optimal graft placement.

2

Sinus Membrane Elevation

A small window is created in the lateral sinus wall. The sinus membrane is carefully elevated using specialized instruments to create space for the graft.

3

Bone Graft Placement

Bio-compatible bone graft material is packed into the space beneath the elevated membrane. The site is then closed with sutures for healing.

4

Healing & Implant Placement

Over 4-8 months, the graft integrates with natural bone, creating sufficient volume for implant placement. Regular check-ups monitor progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia with optional conscious sedation, so you feel no pain during surgery. Post-operative discomfort is typically mild to moderate and well-controlled with prescribed medications. Most patients report less discomfort than expected.

The bone graft typically needs 4-8 months to mature and integrate with your natural bone before implants can be placed. In some cases where adequate initial stability is achievable, implants may be placed simultaneously with the sinus lift, reducing overall treatment time.

Sinus lift surgery is a well-established procedure with a high success rate. Rare complications include sinus membrane perforation, infection, or graft failure. Our use of 3D planning, piezosurgery, and microsurgical techniques minimizes these risks significantly.
