What Causes Dark Gums?

Gum hyperpigmentation occurs when excess melanin deposits cause dark brown, black, or blotchy patches on the gum tissue. This is most commonly a genetic trait seen across various ethnicities, but it can also be caused by smoking, certain medications (such as antimalarials or minocycline), or systemic conditions.

While dark gums are medically harmless, many patients feel self-conscious about their appearance, especially when pigmentation contrasts sharply with white teeth. Laser gum depigmentation at Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry safely and effectively removes the pigmented layer, revealing the naturally pink connective tissue beneath for a more uniform, aesthetically pleasing smile.

The De-Pigmentation Process

1

Assessment & Photography

We document the extent and pattern of pigmentation with clinical photographs and assess your overall gum health before treatment.

2

Anaesthesia

Topical anaesthetic gel or local anaesthesia is applied to ensure complete comfort during the laser procedure.

3

Laser De-Pigmentation

A diode laser is carefully passed over the pigmented areas, ablating the melanin-containing surface layer. The laser simultaneously sterilises the tissue.

4

Healing & Results

New, pink epithelium regenerates within 10-14 days. The gums heal to a natural, uniform pink colour that enhances your entire smile.
