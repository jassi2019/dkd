When Is Tooth Extraction Necessary?

While our priority is always to save your natural teeth, extraction becomes necessary when a tooth is severely decayed, fractured beyond repair, impacted, or causing crowding. Our oral surgeons use minimally invasive techniques combined with advanced anaesthesia protocols to ensure a completely pain-free experience.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, we offer both simple extractions for visible teeth and surgical extractions for impacted or broken teeth. Each case is carefully evaluated using digital X-rays and 3D CBCT scans to plan the safest, most efficient approach.

Our Extraction Process

A systematic approach ensuring safety and comfort at every step

1

Diagnosis & Imaging

Digital X-rays and 3D CBCT scans to evaluate the tooth, roots, and surrounding bone structure for precise treatment planning.

2

Anaesthesia & Preparation

Effective local anaesthesia is administered to completely numb the area. Sedation options are available for anxious patients.

3

Gentle Extraction

The tooth is carefully loosened and removed using specialized instruments. Surgical cases involve a small incision for access.

4

Healing & Recovery

The site is cleaned, sutured if needed, and you receive detailed aftercare instructions. Follow-up ensures proper healing.
