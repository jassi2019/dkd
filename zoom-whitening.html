Zoom AP Teeth Whitening

About This Treatment

The World's Most Popular Whitening System

Philips Zoom Advanced Power (AP) is the world's number one requested in-office whitening system, trusted by millions of patients and dental professionals worldwide. This advanced system uses a specially formulated hydrogen peroxide gel activated by the proprietary Zoom LED lamp to break down deep-set stains and discolouration at the molecular level.

What sets Zoom AP apart from generic whitening is its patented WhiteSpeed technology that delivers maximum whitening with minimal sensitivity. The gel formula contains ACP (amorphous calcium phosphate) that protects enamel and reduces sensitivity during and after treatment. Most patients achieve results 6–8 shades brighter in a single 45-minute session.

  • Patented ACP formula minimises sensitivity

How It Works

The Zoom Whitening Process

1

Pre-Treatment Check

Your dentist examines your teeth for cavities and gum health, performs a shade assessment, and ensures you are a suitable candidate for Zoom whitening.

2

Gum & Lip Protection

A protective barrier is applied to cover your gums, lips, and cheeks. Only the tooth surfaces are exposed, ensuring safe and targeted whitening.

3

Zoom Gel + LED Activation

Zoom whitening gel is applied to your teeth and activated by the Zoom LED lamp. Three 15-minute cycles deliver progressively brighter results.

4

Fluoride & Take-Home Kit

A fluoride treatment strengthens and protects your enamel. You receive custom take-home whitening trays for periodic touch-ups to maintain your results.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Zoom different from regular whitening?

Zoom AP uses a proprietary gel formula with built-in ACP (amorphous calcium phosphate) that protects enamel and reduces sensitivity — a common drawback of generic whitening systems. The Zoom LED lamp is specifically calibrated to activate this gel for maximum effectiveness. Clinical studies show Zoom delivers whiter results with less post-treatment sensitivity than most other systems.

How long do Zoom whitening results last?

Zoom results typically last 1–2 years depending on your diet and lifestyle habits. Using the included take-home trays for periodic touch-ups can extend results even further. Avoiding excessive consumption of staining substances like coffee, red wine, and tobacco helps maintain brightness.

Is Zoom whitening safe for enamel?

Yes, Zoom whitening is completely safe for dental enamel when performed by a qualified dentist. The ACP in the Zoom formula actually helps remineralise and protect your enamel during treatment. Studies have shown no adverse effects on enamel microhardness or surface structure after Zoom whitening.
