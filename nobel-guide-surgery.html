What Is Nobel Guide Surgery?

NobelGuide is Nobel Biocare's proprietary computer-guided implant surgery system. It uses advanced 3D imaging (CBCT scans) combined with sophisticated software to virtually plan every detail of your implant surgery before a single incision is made. A custom surgical guide — essentially a 3D-printed template — is then fabricated to transfer this digital plan directly into your mouth with sub-millimetre accuracy.

This technology allows flapless surgery in many cases, meaning the implant is placed through a tiny punch in the gum rather than a traditional incision. The result is dramatically less trauma, minimal bleeding, reduced swelling, and significantly faster healing.

Benefits of Nobel Guide Surgery

Sub-millimetre precision in implant positioning
Minimally invasive — often flapless, no sutures
Dramatically reduced surgery time (15-20 min per implant)
Less pain, swelling, and faster recovery
Predictable outcomes — surgery is rehearsed digitally first
Ideal for complex cases and anxious patients

The Treatment Process

How computer-guided surgery ensures perfect results

1

3D Imaging & Scanning

A CBCT scan captures a detailed 3D map of your jawbone, nerves, sinuses, and remaining teeth. Digital impressions record your soft tissue and bite relationship.

2

Virtual Surgery Planning

Using NobelClinician software, the implantologist plans the exact position, angle, and depth of each implant on a virtual 3D model of your jaw — optimising for bone quality and prosthetic outcome.

3

Surgical Guide Fabrication

A custom 3D-printed surgical template is manufactured from the digital plan. This guide snaps onto your teeth or gums and directs the drill to the exact planned position.

4

Guided Implant Placement

Using the surgical guide, implants are placed with pinpoint accuracy through minimally invasive punch incisions. The entire procedure is faster, gentler, and more predictable.
