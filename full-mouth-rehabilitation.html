What Is Full Mouth Rehabilitation?

Full mouth rehabilitation is a comprehensive treatment approach that addresses every aspect of your oral health. It combines multiple dental procedures — implants, crowns, bridges, veneers, gum treatment, and bite correction — into a coordinated plan that restores your entire mouth to optimal function, health, and beauty.

This treatment is ideal for patients who have suffered extensive tooth loss, severe wear, trauma, or long-standing neglect. Our multidisciplinary team of implantologists, prosthodontists, periodontists, and cosmetic dentists collaborate to design a master plan tailored to your unique situation.

Benefits of Full Mouth Rehabilitation

Complete restoration of all teeth — upper and lower arches
Corrects bite alignment and jaw function
Eliminates chronic pain from damaged or missing teeth
Dramatic aesthetic transformation — a brand-new smile
Multidisciplinary approach by a team of specialists
Long-term solution with lasting results

The Treatment Process

A carefully phased approach to rebuilding your smile

1

Comprehensive Diagnosis

Full oral examination, 3D CBCT scans, digital impressions, bite analysis, and photographs. Every detail is documented to create your master treatment plan.

2

Foundation Preparation

Gum disease treatment, extractions of hopeless teeth, bone grafting where needed, and root canal therapy on salvageable teeth to create a healthy foundation.

3

Implant Placement & Restoration

Strategic implant placement followed by custom crowns, bridges, and veneers. A temporary prosthesis ensures you never go without teeth during treatment.

4

Final Prosthetics & Refinement

Permanent, precision-crafted restorations are placed. Bite is fine-tuned, aesthetics perfected, and a maintenance plan is designed for lifelong results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Depending on complexity, treatment spans 3-12 months and is completed in phases. You receive temporary restorations at each stage so you always have functional, presentable teeth throughout the process.

All procedures are performed under appropriate anaesthesia with sedation options available for anxious patients. Post-operative discomfort is managed with prescribed medications. Most patients report the experience is far more comfortable than they expected.

Patients with multiple missing teeth, extensive decay, severely worn teeth, chronic jaw pain, or those who have not visited a dentist for many years are excellent candidates. A thorough evaluation will determine the best approach for your situation.

We offer accelerated protocols for international patients. With advanced planning, many cases can be completed in 2-3 visits spread over a few months. Our dental tourism team handles all logistics including accommodation recommendations.
