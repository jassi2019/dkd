What Is a Panoramic X-Ray (OPG)?

An OPG (Orthopantomogram) is a panoramic dental X-ray that captures a single, wide-angle image of your entire mouth in one sweep. The image shows both the upper and lower jaws, all teeth (including unerupted wisdom teeth), the temporomandibular joints (TMJ), sinuses, and surrounding bone structure.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, our digital panoramic machine produces high-quality images with significantly less radiation than traditional panoramic film systems. The scan takes just 15-20 seconds and is completely painless. It is an indispensable diagnostic tool for implant planning, orthodontic assessment, wisdom tooth evaluation, and comprehensive oral health screening.

Benefits of Digital OPG

Complete view of both jaws in one image
Essential for implant and orthodontic planning
Detects cysts, tumours, and jaw pathologies
Evaluates wisdom tooth position and impaction
Low radiation — safer than multiple intraoral films
Quick scan — only 15-20 seconds

The OPG Scan Process

1

Preparation

You remove any jewellery, glasses, or metal accessories. A lead apron is placed for radiation protection.

2

Positioning

You stand in the machine, bite gently on a positioning guide, and hold the handles for stability.

3

Panoramic Scan

The arm of the machine rotates slowly around your head for 15-20 seconds, capturing the full panoramic image.

4

Instant Analysis

The digital image appears on screen immediately. Your dentist reviews it with you and explains all findings.
