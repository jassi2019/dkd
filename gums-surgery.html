Gums Surgery

Advanced surgical treatment for severe gum disease with bone regeneration

Home Treatments Gums Treatment Gums Surgery

When Is Gum Surgery Needed?

When periodontal disease progresses beyond what deep cleaning can manage, surgical intervention becomes necessary to save your teeth. Advanced periodontitis creates deep pockets (6mm or greater) where bacteria thrive beyond the reach of non-surgical instruments, causing progressive bone destruction.

At Dr. Kathuria's Dentistry, our fellowship-trained periodontists perform flap surgery, bone grafting, and guided tissue regeneration (GTR) using microsurgical techniques. These procedures reduce pocket depth, regenerate lost bone, and create an environment where your gums can heal and reattach to the teeth.

Microsurgical precision with magnification loupes
Bone grafting with synthetic and allograft materials
Guided tissue regeneration (GTR) with barrier membranes
Laser-assisted surgery for reduced post-operative pain
Platelet-rich fibrin (PRF) for accelerated healing
Save teeth that would otherwise need extraction

The Surgical Process

1

Pre-Surgical Planning

3D CBCT scans and periodontal charting provide a detailed map of bone loss patterns to guide the surgical approach.

2

Flap Elevation

Under local anaesthesia, gum tissue is gently reflected to expose the root surfaces and underlying bone defects for direct access.

3

Debridement & Grafting

All diseased tissue and calculus are removed. Bone graft material and regenerative membranes are placed in defect areas to stimulate regrowth.

4

Suturing & Healing

Gum tissue is repositioned and sutured precisely. A structured healing protocol with medications and follow-up visits ensures optimal recovery.
